Reptile

Reptiles are air-breathing vertebrates. Their skin are cover by scales , bony plates or combine both of them.They are include crocodiles , snakes , lizards , turtles and more.All regularly shed the outer layer of their skin. Most of them have cold blood and lay eggs.They usually lay 1 to about 50 eggs a time.Their body are cover with scales or hard parts. Most of the reptiles have habitats called camouflage including Lizard, Chameleons, Vipers , Snake, Agamas, Draco, Geckos, Uroplatus and more.

Amphibians

Amphibians are small vertebrates that need water, or a moist environment, to survive. The species in this group include frogs, toads, salamanders, and newts. All can breathe and absorb water through their very thin skin. Amphibians also have special skin glands that produce useful proteins. Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates that start their life in water before sprouting legs and walking on land. They have no scales, and they are carnivores. During reproduction, they release eggs that are fertilized outside of the body. Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates (vertebrates that have backbones) that don't have scales. They live part of their lives in water and part on land. American Bullfrog.
